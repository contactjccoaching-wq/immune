SP Alone: Same Impact Regardless of Model
Opus + SP only (30.6) ≈ Sonnet + SP only (31.1) ≈ Opus + Immune (31.1). All three conditions cluster at ~31/40. SP principles give the same boost whether on Sonnet or Opus. This means SP alone saturates around 31 — you need immune memory to break through. Without learning, Opus + SP actually declines over rounds (34→29) due to task variance.
SP + Immune: Additive, Not Redundant
Adding immune on top of SP boosts avg from 30.6 to 34.8 (+14%). The immune system catches what principles miss: domain-specific patterns like HMAC timing attacks, CSRF double-submit, and JWT refresh rotation. SP sets the floor high; immune raises the ceiling. The combination is consistently better than either alone.
Full Memory: The Ceiling at 37.0
Pre-loading all 73 AB + 59 CS eliminates the learning curve: 37/40 from R1, peak 38/40. But it costs +46% tokens vs SP+Immune for only +2.2 avg points. The last 2 points have severe diminishing returns. SP+Immune reaches 94% of this ceiling at 68% of the cost.
Clear Hierarchy: Principles > Patterns > Model
1. SP principles: +13 pts instant (Haiku 17→Sonnet+SP 30)
2. Immune patterns: +4.2 pts progressive (Opus+SP 30.6→SP+Imm 34.8)
3. Model upgrade alone: Sonnet→Opus = +6 pts (25→31)
4. Model upgrade with SP: +0 pts (Sonnet+SP 31.1 ≈ Opus+SP 30.6)
Best ROI: SP + progressive immune learning
